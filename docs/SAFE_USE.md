# Safe Use Policy

Deckplating is an unofficial open-source prototype. It is not approved by the Department of the Navy or Department of Defense. Do not use it on government-furnished equipment or government networks unless authorized by local IT/N6.

## Purpose

Deckplating supports technical demonstration of unclassified, non-sensitive Religious Ministry Team coverage awareness. It helps a team see where routine presence has occurred, where coverage is stale, and what broad unit or location may need attention.

## Approved Demonstration Use

Approved demonstration use means controlled demonstration workspaces with scrubbed or low-sensitivity administrative coverage metadata, pending local IT/N6, privacy, records, OPSEC, and command guidance.

## Not Approved for Operational Use Unless Authorized

Deckplating is not a Navy-approved operational application. It is not a system of record, counseling record, medical tracker, case-management tool, CUI system, or classified system. Do not use it for operational Navy workflows unless authorized.

## Data Allowed

- workspace name or local command label, if non-sensitive
- broad area names
- public/general buildings or locations already broadly identifiable
- unit, department, division, or tenant-command names, when not sensitive
- team member display names limited to practical ministry workflow identity, such as rank/last name or role/name
- generic visit/check-in timestamps
- generic coverage status

## Data Prohibited

- CUI
- classified information
- counseling notes
- case management data
- medical details
- incident details
- family information
- home addresses
- phone numbers
- dates of birth
- private email addresses
- setup codes and passphrases in any form, screenshot, feedback, or report
- sensitive operational locations
- SCIFs or restricted rooms
- deployed/theater operational locations
- official records

## Location Rules

Map only public/general locations. Do not map restricted rooms, SCIFs, residences, deployed locations, sensitive operational spaces, or any location that should not be broadly visible. When uncertain, leave the location unmapped and use manual check-in.

## Team Member Display-Name Rules

Display names are allowed because a practical coverage workflow needs local accountability. Use the minimum practical identity, such as rank/last name or role/name. Do not enter phone numbers, dates of birth, family details, private contact information, or sensitive identifiers.

## Email/Contact Rules

Official administrative contact email may be collected only when necessary for approval coordination and notifications. Avoid personal email where possible. Do not collect phone numbers. Do not send setup codes or passphrases through feedback forms, screenshots, public docs, or chat.

Deckplating is designed to avoid sensitive PII and to minimize low-sensitivity administrative identity/contact data. Team display names and official contact emails, if used, should be limited to what is necessary for access coordination and local accountability.

## Records Boundary

Deckplating data is demonstration coverage metadata, not an official record. Do not use Deckplating to create, store, replace, transmit, or summarize official records. Use command-approved systems and records processes for official business.

## Stop Conditions

Stop use and seek guidance if anyone tries to enter prohibited data, use the app for real case management, map sensitive spaces, capture screenshots with production values, use government networks without authorization, or treat Deckplating as a Navy-approved operational system.

## N6/Privacy/Records Review Questions

- Is the hosting model authorized for the intended demonstration?
- Are the app, network path, and devices authorized for use?
- Are display names and administrative contact emails acceptable for this purpose?
- What retention period is appropriate for demonstration metadata?
- Are map locations sufficiently general and non-sensitive?
- Is any data category CUI, official record material, or otherwise restricted?
- Is the notification method approved for sending workspace links or setup information?
- What process should stop or delete a workspace after the demonstration?
